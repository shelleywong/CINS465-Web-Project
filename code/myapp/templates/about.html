Our Story

The purpose of homeroom is simple: be a safe place for professors, students, tutors and TAs to get to know each other and communicate better outside of the classroom. With ever-increasing class sizes, it can be challenging to make sure everyone is on the same page. Professors have a difficult time getting to know their students. Office hours rarely work for everyone. We need a better way to connect with our classes.

Homeroom is a concept used in schools throughout the world. It generally serves as an advisory classroom session, for teachers to record attendance and make announcements. However, homeroom does not exist for everyone, and it is virtually non-existent in the world of post secondary education.

Is homeroom necessary? Maybe not. But it is a worthwhile idea to give students a home base, make sure everyone is on the same page and provide a more relaxed environment than in a standard academic lecture. Students and teachers can appreciate having an easy way to hold discussions and get to know each other better. When face-to-face, one-on-one meetings are next to impossible, homeroom is a gathering place, where teachers and students can start building a community where everyone feels supported.

So feel free to join us! Set up a group for your class. Start a discussion. Make a last minute announcement. And try to learn all the names of the students who sit in that same room every week. Homeroom is not the solution to every problem in academia. But it is a good start.
